\section{Analysis and Discussion}
Energy consumption per round is tracked over 300 simulation rounds. ACLIF's adaptive
power control ($a_\mathrm{pwr} \in \{-1,0,+1\}$) reduces unnecessary transmission
energy while the energy-balanced routing mode ($a_\mathrm{route}=1$) distributes
relay load to nodes with higher residual energy. By round 200, ACLIF retains
22\% more mean residual energy than EEHCT and 31\% more than E-GLBR.
Average energy per round is reduced by 24.6\% vs E-GLBR and 33.2\% vs FACSO.

\subsubsection*{Energy Model}
\begin{align}
E_\mathrm{tx}(k,d_{ij}) &= k E_\mathrm{elec} + k\varepsilon_\mathrm{amp} d_{ij}^\alpha \\
E_i(t+1) &= E_i(t) - E_\mathrm{tx}(k,d_{ij}) - E_\mathrm{rx}(k)
\end{align}
